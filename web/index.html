Quick Meanings

SOH is current capacity compared to original capacity.
RUL is cycles left before the battery hits end-of-life.
Risk flags unusual cycles vs typical behavior.

Definitions

SOH (State of Health) If the battery started at 2.0 Ah and now delivers 1.6 Ah, SOH = 1.6 / 2.0 = 80%. This is calculated from the dataset for past cycles, and also predicted for upcoming cycles.

RUL (Remaining Useful Life) End-of-life (EOL) is when SOH drops below a threshold (default 80%). RUL is the number of cycles left until that point. We show both calculated history and predictions.

Prediction vs Calculation Calculations use measured capacity for completed cycles. Predictions estimate SOH and RUL from patterns in voltage, current, temperature, and earlier cycles to give a forward-looking view.

How The Model Works

Models Two Random Forest regressors predict SOH and RUL. An Isolation Forest flags risky cycles (anomaly score).

Training The model learns from NASA discharge cycles using engineered features like voltage, current, temperature, cycle count, rolling capacity averages, and fade rate. One battery is held out for testing.

Prediction For each cycle, the model uses those features to estimate SOH and RUL and assigns a risk level based on how unusual the cycle looks compared to the training data.

Practical Guidance

Warranty Awareness Many EV warranties cover battery capacity down to a threshold (often around 70% SOH). Check your OEM terms for exact limits and time/mileage coverage.

Service Signals Consider a service check if SOH drops quickly, risk stays high for several cycles, or RUL falls sharply.

Owner Tips Avoid frequent fast charging, extreme heat, and deep discharges to slow long-term degradation.
